## What I learned

### What is cybersecurity?
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting digital systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, and damage.

Its main goal is to ensure the **confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA)** of information while identifying, preventing, and responding to security risks and vulnerabilities.

---

### Types of hackers
- **White Hat** → ethical hackers who improve security by finding vulnerabilities  
- **Black Hat** → malicious attackers who exploit systems for personal gain  
- **Grey Hat** → operates between ethical and unethical behavior, depending on context  

---

### Offensive vs Defensive security
- **Offensive Security** → focuses on simulating attacks to identify vulnerabilities (e.g., penetration testing), red teamning
- **Defensive Security** → focuses on protecting systems, detecting threats, and responding to incidents, blue teaming

---

### SOC and SIEM
During this module, I also learned about Security Operations Centers (SOC) and Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems.

A **SOC (Security Operations Center)** is a team responsible for continuously monitoring systems, detecting threats, and responding to security incidents in real time.

A **SIEM system** collects and analyzes log data from multiple sources (servers, applications, network devices) to detect suspicious activity, correlate events, and help identify potential security threats.

These two concepts are essential in real-world defensive cybersecurity operations.

---

### Cybersecurity career paths
This module also introduced different roles within the cybersecurity field:

- **Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker)** → simulates attacks to find vulnerabilities in systems  
- **Security Analyst** → monitors systems and investigates suspicious activity  
- **Incident Responder** → handles and responds to active security incidents  
- **Cloud Security Engineer** → secures cloud-based infrastructure and services  
- **Security Engineer** → designs and implements security systems and controls  
- **Digital Forensics Analyst** → investigates cyber incidents and analyzes evidence after attacks  

This helped me understand that cybersecurity is a broad field with multiple specialization paths.

---

## Key insights

- Cybersecurity is not only about hacking, but also about detection, prevention, and response  
- The CIA triad (confidentiality, integrity, availability) is a core principle in security  
- SOC and SIEM systems play a critical role in real-world security monitoring  
- There are many different career paths depending on interest and specialization
